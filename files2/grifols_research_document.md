# Grifols Research Document: Raw Material for Case Study

## Company Overview

**Grifols S.A.** is a Barcelona-headquartered global healthcare company and one of the world's leading manufacturers of plasma-derived medicines. Founded in 1909 by hematologist Josep Antoni Grífols i Roig, it is now the European leader and third-largest globally in blood plasma-based products.

**Workforce:** ~24,000-26,000 employees
**Geographic presence:** Direct presence in 30+ countries, serving 110+ countries
**Listings:** Madrid Stock Exchange (MCE: GRF, GRF.P) and NASDAQ (GRFS)
**Tax domicile:** Ireland

---

## Business Segments

### 1. Biopharma (Core business, ~75-80% of revenue)

The main growth engine. Transforms donated plasma into therapeutic proteins through fractionation.

**Key Products:**
- **Immunoglobulins (IVIG/SCIG):** For primary and secondary immunodeficiencies, neurological disorders. Highest growth potential. New product Yimmugo approved 2024.
- **Albumin:** For intensive care patients and liver disease. Major driver in China.
- **Alpha-1 Antitrypsin (AAT/Prolastin):** For genetic COPD. 900 plasma donations needed to treat one Alpha-1 patient for one year.
- **Clotting factors (Factor VIII, Fibrinogen):** For hemophilia and bleeding disorders.
- **Hyperimmunes:** Specialized immunoglobulins for specific conditions.

**Demand dynamics:**
- IG demand growing 6-10% annually
- Chronic, non-discretionary patient base
- Undersupplied market — plasma-derived products cannot be synthesized in a lab

### 2. Diagnostics (~10% of revenue)

- Blood Typing Solutions (BTS)
- NAT Donor Screening (nucleic acid testing)
- Immunoassays
- ~45% of worldwide blood components tested using Grifols technology
- Reagent pull-through model on installed base

### 3. Bio Supplies (~5% of revenue)

- Biological materials for life-science research
- Clinical trials supplies
- Manufacturing inputs for pharma/diagnostic companies
- Monetizes byproducts from fractionation

## The Plasma Value Chain (Critical for Modeling)

### Plasma Collection

**Network:**
- World's largest private network: 390-400+ plasma centers globally
- ~87 centers in Europe (including Haema in Germany)
- Rest primarily in U.S. (where paid donation is permitted)
- Expanding to Egypt (9 centers, planning 20), Canada (partnership with Canadian Blood Services)

**Unit Economics:**
- Average center collects ~40,000 liters/year (compensated donors)
- Non-compensated centers collect ~10,000-15,000 liters/year
- Cost per liter (CPL) is key operational metric
- CPL peaked July 2022 (post-COVID donor shortage)
- CPL declined 22% from July 2022 peak to end 2023
- CPL declined additional high-single-digits in 2024
- Target CPL: ~$150-200 per liter (down from peak of ~$250+)

**Donor Economics:**
- Donor compensation: ~$50-100 per donation (varies by center, bonuses)
- Donor fees = ~35% of fully-loaded plasma cost
- Other costs (labor, rent, equipment, etc.) = ~65%
- Productivity metric: plasma collections per FTE (improved 32% in recent years)
- Donors can donate up to 2x per week (FDA rules)

### Fractionation (Manufacturing)

- Cold ethanol fractionation process converts pooled plasma into protein fractions
- 9-12 months from donation to finished medicine
- Fractionation capacity: 16-20 million liters annually
- Yield optimization: grams of IgG extracted per liter of plasma
- Biotest acquisition (2022) added European capacity

**Key manufacturing sites:**
- Barcelona, Spain (HQ)
- Los Angeles, USA
- North Carolina, USA
- Ireland
- Biotest facilities (Germany)

### Distribution

- Cold-chain distribution required
- Sell to hospitals, specialty pharmacies, blood centers
- Home infusion increasingly important for SCIG
- China distribution through Shanghai RAAS partnership

## Key M&A History

### Biotest Acquisition (2022)

- **Price:** ~€2bn financed with bond issuance
- **Rationale:** European fractionation capacity, plasma centers (Haema, BPC), R&D pipeline (IgM, Fibrinogen, Trimodulin)
- **Completed:** April 2022
- **Current status:** 96% voting rights, 70% share capital
- **Integration:** "Biotest Next Level" (BNL) project ongoing

### Shanghai RAAS Partnership/Divestiture

- **2020:** Strategic alliance with Shanghai RAAS (leading Chinese plasma company)
- **2023:** Strategic alliance with Haier Group; agreement to sell 20% SRAAS stake for $1.8bn (€1.6bn)
- **June 2024:** Sale completed; proceeds used for debt reduction
- **Extended albumin distribution agreement:** 10 years, potentially up to 20 years
- **Current holding:** 6.6% SRAAS (held as equity investment post-sale)

### Historical Acquisitions

- **Talecris Biotherapeutics (2011):** Major U.S. acquisition, established scale in North America
- **Novartis Diagnostics (2014):** Built diagnostics division
- **Various plasma center roll-ups:** Continuous expansion of collection network

## The Brookfield Take-Private Saga (2024)

### Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Jan 9, 2024 | Gotham City Research publishes short report; stock drops 34% |
| Jan 26, 2024 | Grifols sues Gotham in New York federal court |

| Nov 19, 2024 | Board rejects offer as "significantly undervaluing" the company |
| Nov 27, 2024 | Discussions formally terminated |
| Dec 2024 | Company completes balance sheet improvement with additional €1.3bn notes |

### Key Deal Points

**Proposed Valuation:**
- Enterprise Value: ~€17-18bn (equity €6.45bn + debt ~€11bn)
- EV/EBITDA: ~9-10x on 2024E EBITDA

**Financing Structure (Reported):**
- Deutsche Bank and Banco Santander advising on €11bn debt package
- Would have required refinancing existing debt (Change of Control triggers)
- Bondholders could demand early repayment at premium under existing docs

**Why Board Rejected:**
- Valued company at €6.45bn vs. management's view of fundamental value
- Strong Q3 2024 results (revenue +12% cc in Biopharma)
- Deleveraging progress (leverage improved to ~5.5x)
- Long-term growth prospects (IG market, pipeline, China)

**What Brookfield Saw:**
- Distressed valuation opportunity (post-Gotham attack)
- Strong underlying business fundamentals
- Operational improvement potential
- Deleveraging path via asset sales and FCF generation

## The Gotham City Short Attack (January 2024)

### Core Allegations

1. **EBITDA Manipulation:** Company consolidates earnings from entities it doesn't control (BPC Plasma, Haema), overstating EBITDA

2. **Debt Understated:** True leverage 10x-13x, not reported 6x
   - Factoring should be treated as debt
   - Consolidation of entities with minority partners inflates metrics

3. **Related Party Concerns:**
   - Scranton Enterprises (Grifols family vehicle) and Grifols both consolidate BPC/Haema
   - Undisclosed related-party loans ($95m loan to Scranton in 2018)
   - Non-controlling interests grew from 0% to ~100% of profits (2018-2023)

4. **Cash Flow Access:** Even if EBITDA is real, cash flow from consolidated entities may not be available to service Grifols debt

### Company Response

- "Categorically" denied all allegations
- Called report "false information" and "speculation"
- All related-party transactions disclosed and audited since 2018
- Sued Gotham in U.S. federal court
- KPMG audited 2023 accounts "without exceptions"
- CNMV (Spanish regulator) investigating both sides
- Spain's High Court opened probe into Gotham for potential market manipulation

### Market Impact

- Stock fell 34% on report date (Jan 9, 2024)
- ~$3.8bn market cap wiped out
- Bonds fell 5+ points
- Recovery began after audited accounts released and asset sales closed

## Risk Factors for Case Study

### Operational Risks

1. **Plasma supply dependency:** Cannot synthesize; relies on donors
2. **Donor economics volatility:** Compensation costs swing with labor market
3. **Yield optimization limits:** Physics/biology constrain extraction rates
4. **Manufacturing complexity:** 9-12 month production cycle
5. **Regulatory risk:** FDA, EMA, CNMV oversight

### Financial Risks

1. **High leverage:** ~5-6x currently, targeting <4x
2. **Refinancing risk:** Large maturities in 2027-2028
3. **Interest rate exposure:** Mix of fixed and floating
4. **Currency exposure:** EUR functional, significant USD operations
5. **Working capital intensity:** Long inventory cycle (plasma → finished goods)

### Strategic Risks

1. **Recombinant competition:** Biotech may develop synthetic alternatives
2. **China regulatory/geopolitical:** Significant exposure via SRAAS/Haier
3. **Family governance:** Concentrated control, related-party complexity
4. **Short-seller overhang:** Gotham allegations create ongoing scrutiny

## Why This Works for LBO Case Study

### Characteristics That Make Grifols Ideal

1. **Defensive, essential business:** Patients need plasma therapies; demand is non-discretionary

2. **High barriers to entry:** Plasma collection network, fractionation capacity, regulatory approvals take decades to build

3. **Predictable revenue:** Chronic patient base creates recurring demand

4. **Operational improvement opportunity:** CPL reduction, center optimization, yield improvement all actionable

5. **Complex capital structure:** Senior secured, senior unsecured, multiple currencies, change of control provisions

6. **Real covenant dynamics:** Credit facility leverage tests, secured notes with CoC puts

7. **Sponsor interest validated:** Brookfield's approach (even if failed) confirms PE thesis

8. **Deleveraging story:** Path from ~6x to <4x through operations and asset sales

9. **Multiple exit paths:** Re-IPO, strategic sale, dividend recap post-deleveraging

### Modeling Opportunities

- **Revenue drivers:** Volume (plasma collections, yield) × Price × Mix
- **Cost drivers:** CPL, labor, manufacturing efficiency
- **Working capital:** Long inventory cycle, receivables from healthcare systems
- **Capex:** Maintenance (equipment) vs. Growth (new centers, capacity)
- **Debt structure:** Multiple tranches, currencies, maturities
- **Covenant analysis:** Leverage tests, basket capacity, CoC implications
